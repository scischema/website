About SciSchema

SciSchema is a collaborative, community-driven initiative with a mission to create, maintain, and promote schemas for machine-readable scientific process descriptions.

SciSchema aims to make scientific methods more transparent, comparable, reusable, and machine-actionable. The initiative develops schemas for experimental, computational, and analytical processes across scientific domains.

The project combines domain expertise, semantic technologies, and AI-assisted schema mining workflows. Community discussion takes place through the SciSchema Google Group. You can also contact the project directly at sciknoworg@gmail.com.

The schema mining software supporting this initiative is available as Schema-Miner.

Organization

SciSchema is coordinated as an open scientific community initiative. The day-to-day direction of the project, including schema design principles, community organization, tooling, and publication workflows, is led by Jennifer D'Souza. Technical development is supported by the Schema-Miner software effort.

People

A number of people have made substantial contributions to SciSchema. These include:

  • Jennifer D'Souza is the founder and lead of SciSchema. She leads the project vision, conceptual development, organization, tooling direction, schema design approach, and community-building activities.
  • Sameer Sadruddin is the lead developer of Schema-Miner and contributes to the technical implementation of the SciSchema workflow.
  • Anisa Rula is an early close community collaborator and contributes to the development of SciSchema as a semantic technology and community initiative.
