About SciSchema.org

SciSchema.org is a collaborative, community-driven initiative with a mission to create, maintain, and promote schemas for machine-readable scientific process descriptions.

SciSchema.org aims to make scientific methods more transparent, comparable, reusable, and machine-actionable. The initiative develops schemas for experimental, computational, and analytical processes across scientific domains.

The project combines domain expertise, semantic technologies, and AI-assisted schema mining workflows. Community discussion takes place through the SciSchema.org Google Group. For project-related inquiries, contact: sciknoworg [at] gmail [dot] com

The schema mining software supporting this initiative is available as schema-miner.

Organization

SciSchema.org is coordinated as an open scientific community initiative. The day-to-day direction of the project, including schema design principles, community organization, tooling, and publication workflows, is led by Jennifer D'Souza. Technical development is supported by the schema-miner software effort.

Research Foundations

SciSchema.org emerged from research on AI-assisted scientific schema discovery, schema engineering, and ontology grounding. The initiative builds upon the schema-miner software platform and a series of research efforts investigating how large language models and domain experts can collaboratively develop machine-readable representations of scientific processes.

The development and evaluation of schema-miner and schema-minerpro are documented in the following publications:

  • Sadruddin, S., D'Souza, J., Poupaki, E., Watkins, A., Babaei Giglou, H., Rula, A., ... & Kessels, E. (2025). LLMs4SchemaDiscovery: A Human-in-the-Loop Workflow for Scientific Schema Mining with Large Language Models . In European Semantic Web Conference (ESWC 2025), pp. 244–261. Cham: Springer Nature Switzerland.
  • Sadruddin, S., D'Souza, J., Poupaki, E., Watkins, A., Karasulu, B., Auer, S., ... & Kessels, E. (2026). schema-minerpro: Agentic AI for Ontology Grounding Over LLM-Discovered Scientific Schemas in a Human-in-the-Loop Workflow . Semantic Web, 17(3), 22104968261431521.

People

A number of people have made substantial contributions to SciSchema.org. These include:

  • Jennifer D'Souza founded SciSchema.org and leads the initiative. She oversees the scientific vision, community organization, schema design principles, publication strategy, and overall project direction.
  • Sameer Sadruddin serves as the lead developer of schema-miner, the software platform supporting AI-assisted schema discovery and schema engineering workflows within SciSchema.org.
  • Anisa Rula is an early close community collaborator and contributes to the development of SciSchema.org as a semantic technology and community initiative.
